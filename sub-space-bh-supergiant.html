Supergiant Stars

 

There are some stars that can be 50 or even 100 times more massive than our Sun… we call these stars "supergiant stars"!

These stars are extremely bright and die young. While our Sun will live for billions of years, these supersized stars will live for just millions of years.

These stars, like all stars, are powered by the nuclear fusion of elements in their cores. For most of their lives, these collisions turn hydrogen into helium.

However, once supergiants run out of hydrogen, they start fusing other elements and begin to change temperature, colour and size. Some become red. Can you see red stars when you look at the night sky? You might be looking at a supergiant!

Once these changes start, a star doesn’t have long to live. Something terrible is about to happen... a supernova!

We are all made of atoms, and the different kinds of atoms are called elements. Oxygen, hydrogen, carbon, iron and gold are all elements.

In our daily lives, elements remain unchanged. You cannot easily turn hydrogen into helium or carbon into oxygen.

But in the super hot and super dense core of a star, hydrogen atoms are fused together to become helium atoms. This is nuclear fusion, and this process powers all stars including our Sun.
