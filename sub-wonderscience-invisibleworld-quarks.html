Quarks

We've learnt that all atoms have a nucleus at their centre and that the nucleus is made up of protons and neutrons. Do you think the protons and neutrons made of smaller parts too? And what about those things, are they made up of things that are even smaller? Do you think there a limit to how small these parts can be?

Protons and Neutrons are made from quarks! Each proton is made from three quarks, and each neutron is made from three quarks. The quarks that make up protons and neutrons are given funny names. They're called "up" quarks and "down" quarks. A proton is made from two "up" quarks and one "down" quarks. A neutron is made from one "up" quark and two "down" quarks.

What makes up the quarks? We don't know yet, although we're working on finding out the answer. Perhaps you would like to become a scientist one day, and find out what makes up quarks!
