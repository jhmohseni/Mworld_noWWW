Walk outside, close your eyes and feel the sun shining on your face. This sunlight is made from photons that travelled through almost one hundred and fifty million kilometres of space, before being absorbed by your skin!

Photons from the sun are not the only particles to arrive on Earth from outer space. Many of the Particle Zoo's particles arrive from the sun and other stars. Lots of other things in outer space send particles to the Earth. These particles, arriving on Earth from outer space, are called cosmic rays.

Hold out your hand. At any one time, billions of cosmic ray particles from the all across the universe are travelling straight through your hand! The particles in the cosmic rays pass straight through you without you noticing. Most continue to travel straight through the Earth. Scientists learn a lot about outer space from studying cosmic rays.
