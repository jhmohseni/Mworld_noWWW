Even Smaller

Is there anything that can act as a 'bionic eye' to take pictures of things smaller than atoms? Nobody has yet found a clever way to do this! So, how do we 'see' things that are smaller than atoms?

The answer is: we can see them indirectly. Suppose you were invisible, and standing on a crowded train. Other people would know you were there, because they would bump into you! They'd know indirectly that you were there, because of the effect of you bumping into them, even though they couldn't see you.

Another example; imagine you have a cold. Sneezing and your sore throat indirectly let you know you have a cold, even though the virus causing your cold is too tiny for you to see. The same thing happens with atoms! We can't see them directly, but we can see them indirectly!
