Bubble Clues

Imagine that some soap bubbles are floating past you. From seeing the bubbles, you know someone nearby must be making them, even if you can't see the person.

Particles such as neutrons and protons and electrons can make bubbles too! When one of these very tiny particles passes through certain liquids, it makes little bubbles that can be photographed. Scientists do this with something called a bubble chamber. Just as you can indirectly 'see' an invisible person by the people they bump into, you can indirectly 'see' tiny moving particles such as neutrons by the trail of bubbles they cause in a bubble chamber.

Images from these bubbles give a lot of information about the Particle Zoo. Many of the particles in the Particle Zoo were first discovered using bubble chambers. There are other instruments which also give indirect pictures of particle trails, but the pictures they give are not as pretty as bubble-chamber pictures!
