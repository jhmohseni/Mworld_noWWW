Buzzing Electron

Electrons are like tiny bees that buzz around the nucleus of the atom. Every atom has electrons buzzing around it. The more electrons an atom has, the bigger the atom is.

Sometimes the electrons leave the atom, just like a bee can fly away from a flower. When an electron leaves an atom it might attach itself to another atom, or it might roam free!

Electrons are one of the tiniest things we know of. They're everywhere even though an individual electron is so small it's invisible.
