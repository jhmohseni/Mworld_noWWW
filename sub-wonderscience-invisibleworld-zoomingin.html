Zooming In

No matter how hard you stare at your fingertip, your eyes are not powerful enough to see the individual atoms from which it is made. One way to see them is to use a microscope. Microscopes are like a "bionic eye" - making our eyes more powerful when we peer through them.

We can see individual atoms if we look through a special microscope called an electron microscope. What if we want to see even smaller things such as the nucleus? Unfortunately, no microscope in the world today is powerful enough to see a nucleus ... but there are other ways to see things the size of the nucleus or even smaller.
